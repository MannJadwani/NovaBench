PHILOSOPHY

The International Typographic Style, or Swiss Style, emerged in Switzerland in the 1950s. It emphasizes cleanliness, readability, and objectivity.

Key principles include asymmetric layouts, grid-based designs, sans-serif typography, and the use of photography over illustration. The movement seeks clarity above all else.
